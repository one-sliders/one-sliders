Wine guide

Port wine

Fortified wine from Portugal, usually sweet, ruby-deep and made for slow sipping.

Quick facts

  • Base: fermented grapes
  • Alcohol: usually 19-22% ABV
  • Serve: small pours, lightly chilled for tawny or white styles
  • Taste map: dark fruit, sweetness, tannin, spirit warmth and age
  • Classic matches: blue cheese, chocolate, nuts and dried fruit

How to read it

  1. Start with the color. Ruby port points toward fresh dark fruit, while tawny port leans into nuts, caramel and dried fruit.
  2. Smell before tasting. Spirit warmth is part of the style, but good port should still show fruit, spice or age rather than only alcohol.
  3. Pour small. Port is stronger than table wine, so a modest glass keeps the sweetness and warmth in balance.
  4. Match the style to the moment. Ruby feels bold and fruity, tawny is slower and nutty, and vintage port is structured for aging.
