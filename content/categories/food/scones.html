Recipe

Scones

British afternoon-tea scones with a tender crumb, jam and clotted cream.

Ingredients

  • 350 g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 85 g cold butter
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 175 ml milk
  • 1 egg for brushing
  • Jam and clotted cream

How to make it

  1. Rub cold butter into flour, baking powder and sugar until the mix looks like coarse crumbs. Keep the butter cool for a lighter rise.
  2. Stir in milk just until a soft dough forms. Pat gently to about 3 cm thick instead of kneading hard.
  3. Cut rounds with a floured cutter and place on a lined tray. Brush the tops with beaten egg, keeping the sides clean so they rise evenly.
  4. Bake at 220C for 10-12 minutes until tall and golden. Split while warm and serve with jam and clotted cream.

Serving note

Scones are best the day they are baked. Serve warm, split by hand if possible, with enough jam and cream to keep the crumb soft.
