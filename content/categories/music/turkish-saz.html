Turkish saz

Long-necked lute, poetic song and tea-house intimacy.

Topic

What it is

The saz or bağlama is central to Turkish folk sound: bright, ringing and closely tied to sung poetry. It gives a page intimacy without losing regional character.

History

Where it comes from

Anatolian aşık poet-singers, regional folk songs and modern Turkish recordings all shaped the instrument's public role. The sound can be narrative, devotional, danceable or quiet.
