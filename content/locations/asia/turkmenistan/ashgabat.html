City history

1919From 1919 until 1927, the city was renamed Poltoratsk after a local revolutionary, Pavel Poltoratskiy.
1925Ashgabat is called Aşgabat (transliterated as "Ashgabat") in Turkmen, Russian: Ашхабад, romanized: Ashkhabad in Russian from 1925 to 1991, and عشق‌آباد ('Ešqābād) in Persian.
1991Before 1991, the name was usually spelled Ashkhabad in English, a transliteration of the Russian form.
3rd centuryTurkmen historian Ovez Gundogdiyev believes that the name goes back to the Parthian era, 3rd century BC, deriving from the name of the founder of the Parthian Empire, Arsaces I of Parthia, in Persian.
Modern cityIt has also been variously spelled Ashkhabat and Ashgabad.
Civic roleAlthough the name literally means "city of love" or "city of devotion" in modern Persian, the name might have been modified through folk etymology.
