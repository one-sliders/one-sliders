City history

1299Habibie referred to Singapore as a red dot on a map. Ancient Singapore In 1299, according to the Malay Annals, the Kingdom of Singapura was founded on the island by Sang Nila Utama.
1613In 1613, Portuguese raiders burned down the settlement, and the island faded into obscurity for the next two centuries.
1826In 1826, Singapore became part of the Straits Settlements, then under the jurisdiction of British India.
1867In 1867, the Straits Settlements were separated from British India, coming under the direct control of Britain.
1914Singapore was not greatly affected by the First World War (1914–1918), as the conflict did not spread to Southeast Asia.
1938Costing $60 million and not fully completed in 1938, it was nonetheless the largest dry dock in the world, the third-largest floating dock, and had enough fuel tanks to support the entire British navy.
1946The British Military Administration ended on 1 April 1946, and Singapore became a separate crown colony in its own right, as the Straits Settlements was formally dissolved that year.
1972Singapore Airlines was formed in 1972 and Changi Airport was opened in 1981, replacing the international airport at Paya Lebar.
2024On 15 May 2024, Lawrence Wong became Singapore's fourth prime minister and the first born after independence.
