History

1500Uzbek khanates ruled or strongly influenced the territory before the modern state.
1920Bukharan People's Soviet Republic introduced a new form of state rule.
1924Uzbek SSR placed the territory inside the Soviet administrative system.
1991Uzbekistan became independent from the Soviet Union, marking a key step in modern statehood.
1992Uzbekistan's sovereignty gained formal recognition.
1992The current constitution came into force, setting the basic framework for today's Uzbekistan.

Worth seeing

  • Samarkand: Tamerlane's jewel on the Silk Road, with the Registan square of brilliant tiled madrasas.
  • Bukhara: Ancient holy city of 140 mosques and the Ark fortress, frozen in Silk Road splendour.
  • Khiva: Walled inner city of Itchan Kala, a UNESCO living museum of Islamic architecture.
