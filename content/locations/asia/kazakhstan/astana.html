City history

1830At present, Astana is expanding into the territory of ancient Bozok. Early years (1830–1918) The settlement of Akmoly was established on the Ishim river on 18 June 1830 as the seat of an okrug.
1832In 1832, the settlement was granted town status and named Akmolinsk.
1838In 1838, at the height of the great national and liberation movement headed by Kenesary Khan, the Akmolinsk Fortress was burned.
1869In 1869, Akmolinsk's external district and department were canceled, and Akmolinsk became the center of the newly established Akmolinsk Oblast.
1879In 1879, Major General Dubelt proposed to build a railway between Tyumen and Akmolinsk to the Ministry of Communications of Russia.
1893In 1893, Akmolinsk was an uyezd with a population of 6,428 residents, three churches, five schools and colleges, and three factories. Soviet era (1918–1991) The regional historical museum and state archive were founded in.
1942During World War II, in connection with the formation of the Polish Anders' Army, a Polish diplomatic post was located in the city from February to July 1942.
1954In 1954, Northern Kazakh SSR oblasts became a territory of the Virgin Lands Campaign, in order to turn the region into a second grain producer for the Soviet Union.
1971In 1971, the Tselinniy Krai was abolished and Tselinograd became the oblast's new capital. Contemporary era (1991–present) After the dissolution of the Soviet Union and the consequent independence of Kazakhstan, the city's name was.
1998After the capital of Kazakhstan was moved from Almaty to Akmola on 10 December 1997, the city was consequently renamed Astana in 1998.
