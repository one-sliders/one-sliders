1681The earliest documented mention of Al Bidda was made in 1681 by the Carmelite Convent, in an account that chronicles several settlements in Qatar.
1765Carsten Niebuhr, a German explorer who visited the Arabian Peninsula, created one of the first maps to depict the settlement in 1765, in which he labelled it as 'Guttur'.
1843Isa bin Tarif, a powerful tribal chief from the Al Bin Ali tribe, moved to Doha in May 1843.
1868The joint Bahraini-Abu Dhabi incursion and subsequent Qatari counterattack prompted the British political agent, Colonel Lewis Pelly, to impose a settlement in 1868.
1871In December 1871, the Ottomans established a presence in the country with 100 of their troops occupying the Musallam fort in Doha.
1872The Ottoman commander, Major Ömer Bey, compiled a report on Al Bidda in January 1872, stating that it was an "administrative centre" with around 1,000 houses and 4,000 inhabitants.
1915Ottoman presence in the peninsula ceased, when in August 1915, the Ottoman fort in Al Bidda was evacuated shortly after the start of World War I.
1952The first formal boys' school was established in Doha in 1952, followed three years later by the establishment of a girls' school.
1973In 1973, the University of Qatar was opened by emiri decree, and in 1975, the Qatar National Museum opened in what was originally the ruler's palace.
1993In 1993, the Qatar Open became the first major sports event to be hosted in the city.
