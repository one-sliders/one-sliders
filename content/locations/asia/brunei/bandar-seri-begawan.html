1910By 1910, Chinese immigrants had opened shops, further establishing the colony on land.
1911In 1911 the water village, largely populated by Malay Muslim and a small Kedayan community in nearby areas, was home to many houses built over water.
1920In 1920, the area was officially designated as Brunei's capital and municipal territory.
1921Later on 1 January 1921, the Brunei Town Sanitary Board (BTSB) was established to oversee its development.
