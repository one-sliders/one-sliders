City history

1294Mangrai founded Chiang Mai in 1294 or 1296 on a site that the Lawa people called Wiang Nopburi.
1556With the decline of Lan Na, Chiang Mai lost importance and was occupied by the Toungoo Empire in 1556.
1774It formally became part of the Thonburi Kingdom in 1774, by an agreement with Kawila, after the Thonburi king Taksin helped drive out the Burmese.
1983First covering just 17.5 square kilometres (6.8 sq mi), the city was enlarged to 40.2 square kilometres (15.5 sq mi) on 5 April 1983.
1989The first revision of such an updated urban area (Thai: เขตเมือง) was published in 1989, with the second one following in 1999.
2006In May 2006, Chiang Mai was the site of the Chiang Mai Initiative, concluded between ASEAN and the "ASEAN+3" countries, (China, Japan, and South Korea).
2012The third revision from 2012 expands the old municipal city border inside Muaeng Chiang Mai district to Mae Rim in the north, San Kamphaeng and Doi Saket in the east, Hang Dong and Saraphi.
2014Chiang Mai was one of two tourist destinations in Thailand on TripAdvisor's 2014 list of "25 Best Destinations in the World", where it stood at number 24.
2022As of 2022, a fourth revision of Chiang Mai's urban area is under way and currently up for debate by the public.
2023To reflect the city's growth beyond the municipal borders, official government documents by the Chiang Mai Provincial Administrative Organization and the Department of Public Works and Town & Country Planning Archived 2023-03-28 at the.
GrowthThe city succeeded Chiang Rai as the capital of Lan Na.
Modern cityPhayu enlarged and fortified it and built Wat Phra Singh in honor of his father, Khamfu.
Regional roleChiang Mai is also the place where the new idol group CGM48 was founded. Emblem The city emblem shows the stupa at Wat Phra That Doi Suthep in its center.
Regional roleChiang Mai Municipality has now become a small part of the current city-scape, making up only most parts of the capital district Amphoe Mueang Chiang Mai in the inner city.
