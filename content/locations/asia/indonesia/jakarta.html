City history

1513In 1513, Portuguese authorities in Malacca sent a fleet to Java to obtain spices.
1522Their involvement at Sunda Kelapa became more direct in 1522, when the Sunda Kingdom concluded an agreement with Portugal.
1619After taking control of Jayakarta in 1619, the Dutch East India Company (VOC) rebuilt the settlement as Batavia, a name referring to the Batavi, whom the Dutch regarded as their ancestors.
1741Survivors were barred from living inside the city walls, and in 1741, the VOC designated a Chinese settlement at Diestpoort, south of Batavia, in the area later known as Glodok.
1942Dutch colonial rule ended in March 1942, when Japanese forces captured Batavia during the Second World War and renamed the city Jakarta. Jakarta in independent Indonesia Indonesia's independence was proclaimed in Jakarta on 17.
1946Republican leaders moved the temporary capital to Yogyakarta in early 1946 after British troops entered Jakarta.
1964In 1964, Jakarta was legally designated a Special Capital Region (Daerah Khusus Ibukota, DKI) with administrative status equivalent to a province.
2024A 2024 law gives Jakarta a new special regional framework after the capital is moved, with an emphasis on its role as an economic centre and global city.
