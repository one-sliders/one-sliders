City history

1520sPortuguese and Spanish interest in the island increased in the 1520s, with regular trade established by 1524.
1769In 1769, as Lifau came under the increasing influence of powerful local families collectively known as the Topasses, the Portuguese governor António José Teles de Meneses moved the administration and 1,200 people east to.
1834Significant construction was undertaken under governor José Maria Marques, who arrived in 1834 and rebuilt the settlement along a grid.
1844In 1844 Timor, along with Macau and Solor, was removed from the jurisdiction of Portuguese India, with the three areas becoming a new Portuguese province.
1850A few years later in 1850, Portuguese Timor was removed from the jurisdiction of the governor of Macau, before being returned to the jurisdiction of Portuguese India in 1856.
1933The main church was demolished in 1933, and a new cathedral opened in its place in 1937. (This cathedral was later destroyed by Allied bombing in the Second World War.) Four distinct residential districts.
1940As the administrative structures developed, Dili became part of the Dili municipality in 1940, the first municipal administration to be created.
1951Portuguese Timor became a full part of Following the initial post-war reconstruction of Dili's immediately critical infrastructure, an urban plan was developed in 1951 covering urban layout, road development, zoning, and building regulations.
1963The Portuguese Overseas Organic Law of 1963 created the first Legislative Council of the territory, which was given some of the powers formerly held by the governor.
1974The 1974 Carnation Revolution in Portugal created immediate change in East Timor, with new political parties forming with the goal of independence from Portugal.
1976On 17 July 1976, Indonesia annexed East Timor, which it designated its 27th province.
2009In 2009 the area around the Indonesian-built Integration Monument was redesignated as the 5 May Park, the date upon which it was agreed East Timor could hold its independence referendum.
1960sA 1960s hotel, Hotel Turismo, which had been a site of independence activities, was demolished in 2010 and rebuilt.
Regional roleStructures and monuments built during this time include the Immaculate Conception Cathedral, the Integration Statue commemorating the end of Portuguese rule, and the Cristo Rei of Dili.
