City history

200A riot involving 200 to 300 people broke out after a police officer knocked down a woman and this resulted in the destruction of the police station.
1722The modern capital, Kingstown, was founded by French settlers shortly after 1722, although Saint Vincent was under British rule for 196 years before its independence.
1765The botanical garden, conceived in 1765, is one of the oldest in the Western Hemisphere.
1795During the Second Carib War the fort at Dorsetshire Hill, just outside the city, was taken by rebels on 12 March 1795.
1841In 1841, three militia men fired their guns into the air to celebrate the end of their duty.
1866Plans for water supply were conceived in 1866, after a large fire destroyed large sections of the town, but this was not completed until 1871.
2025Minister of Finance Camillo Gonsalves announced in 2025 that the government was going to rename areas of the country as part of a decolonization project.
