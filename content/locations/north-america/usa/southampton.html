Southampton is the crown jewel of the Hamptons, known for its white-sand beaches, luxury estates, upscale dining and relaxed coastal lifestyle. Located on Long Island, it offers one of America's most desirable seaside escapes just a short journey from New York City.

City Snapshot

PlaceSouthampton anchors the western Hamptons on Long Island's South Fork.
BeachesCooper's Beach and nearby ocean beaches shape the classic summer trip.
CultureVillage streets, Parrish Art Museum and East End galleries add more than beach time.

Why go

  • Beach weekends: A premium Long Island seaside escape close enough for a New York City getaway.
  • Village life: Boutiques, dining, galleries and quiet residential streets make the town easy to enjoy without rushing.
  • Event base: The western Hamptons location works for golf weeks, summer events and East End day trips.

Planning questions

What is Southampton famous for?White-sand beaches, Hamptons estates, polished village life, art, golf and summer getaways.
When is the best time to visit?June to September is peak season; late spring and September are calmer and often easier to book.
Which beaches are worth visiting?Start with Cooper's Beach, then compare village ocean beaches and bay-side options based on parking rules.
How far is Southampton from New York City?It is roughly 90 miles east of Manhattan; summer Friday traffic can make the trip much longer.
Where should I stay?Choose Southampton Village for walkability, Hampton Bays or Riverhead for value, and Bridgehampton for events.
What events happen nearby?Golf weeks, summer cultural events, art openings, benefit weekends and Hamptons food events shape peak demand.

Travel Tips

Best time to visit

Summer weekends book out early and traffic can be heavy eastbound on Fridays.

Transport notes

The Long Island Rail Road and Hampton Jitney can be useful alternatives to driving.

Crowds

Beach parking rules vary, so check permits and access before choosing an area.

Weather considerations

Late spring and September are often calmer than peak July and August.
