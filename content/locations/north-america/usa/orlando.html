Orlando city overview

Orlando is a Central Florida city in Orange County, known for a service economy built around tourism, conventions, entertainment and regional growth. Beyond the theme parks, the city has a downtown core around Lake Eola, nearby historic Winter Park, major airport access and a large metro area that shapes Central Florida.

Worth seeing

Walt Disney World Resort

Use Walt Disney World as a full-day planning anchor: hotel area, early entry, park transfers and evening returns can change the best Orlando base.

Universal Orlando Resort

Universal works best when you compare park days with International Drive, airport timing, dining plans and whether you need a car.

International Drive

International Drive is a practical hotel corridor for first visits, restaurants, attractions, shuttles and easier comparison shopping between Orlando bases.

Lake Eola Park

Lake Eola gives Downtown Orlando a lighter local stop, useful when you want a break from theme-park schedules and resort transfers.

Winter Park

Winter Park adds museums, lakes, restaurants and a calmer local base when the trip needs more than the major theme parks.

Kennedy Space Center day trip

Kennedy Space Center is not in Orlando, but it is a common day-trip decision that can affect rental car and hotel planning.
