Best Areas to Stay

Midtown Manhattan

Central for first-time sightseeing, Broadway, major transit and classic landmarks.

Best for: tourism, business, first visits
Lower Manhattan

Good for downtown history, ferries, finance trips and quieter weekend streets.

Best for: business, history, couples
Upper West Side

Residential base near Central Park, museums and family-friendly restaurants.

Best for: families, museums, longer stays
Williamsburg

Brooklyn base for restaurants, nightlife and skyline views with subway access to Manhattan.

Best for: nightlife, food, repeat visits
Long Island City

Queens base with fast subway links and skyline views, often practical for value comparisons.

Best for: value, transit, families
Flushing / Queens

Useful for US Open Tennis trips and excellent food, with longer rides to Manhattan.

Best for: events, food, tennis

Travel Tips

Best time to visit

Spring and fall are the easiest seasons for walking-heavy trips.

Transport notes

Use the subway for most cross-city travel; taxis and rideshare can be slow in Midtown traffic.

Crowds

Hotel prices jump around holidays, fashion weeks, the marathon, tennis and major concerts.

Weather considerations

Winter can be cold and windy; summer can be humid with sudden storms.
