City history

Early settlementNomadic Paleo-Indians traveled to the Las Vegas area 10,000 years ago, leaving behind petroglyphs.
550This canopied five-block area features 12.5 million LED lights and 550,000 watts of sound from dusk until midnight during shows held at the top of each hour.
1829A young Mexican scout named Rafael Rivera is credited as the first non-Native American to encounter the valley, in 1829.
1905Las Vegas was founded as a city in 1905, when 110 acres (45 ha) of land adjacent to the Union Pacific Railroad tracks were auctioned in what would become the downtown area.
1950Renamed Nellis Air Force Base in 1950, it is now home to the United States Air Force Thunderbirds aerobatic team.
1951In 1951, nuclear weapons testing began at the Nevada Test Site, 65 miles (105 km) northwest of Las Vegas.
1955In 1955, the Moulin Rouge Hotel opened and became the first racially integrated casino-hotel in Las Vegas.
1963Residents and visitors were able to witness the mushroom clouds (and were exposed to the fallout) until 1963 when the Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty required that nuclear tests be moved underground.
1995In 1995, the Fremont Street Experience opened in Las Vegas's downtown area.
2012Due to the realization of many revitalization efforts, 2012 was dubbed "The Year of Downtown".
