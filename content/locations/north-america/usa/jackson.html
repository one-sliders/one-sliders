697Of the 2,697,000 acres (1,091,000 ha) in Teton County, 97% are federally or state owned/managed.
1806After being discharged from the Corps of Discovery of the Lewis and Clark Expedition in 1806 at Fort Mandan, in present-day North Dakota, Colter visited Jackson Hole during the winter of 1807/1808.
1871As part of the Hayden Expedition of 1871 and 1872, William Henry Jackson took the first photographs of the Teton Range and Yellowstone.
1872Yellowstone National Park became the first National Park in 1872.
1894The town of Jackson was named in 1894 and incorporated in 1914.
1920The town of Jackson elected an all-woman city government (including town council and mayor, who in turn appointed women to town marshal, town clerk and treasurer) in 1920.

1937The first ski rope tow was built at Teton Pass in 1937 and Snow King Resort was established in 1930.
2009In 2009, the town of Jackson was designated as a Preserve America Community.
OriginsJackson was originally populated by Native American tribes including the Shoshoni, Crow, Blackfeet, Bannock, and Gros Ventre.
1800sIn the early 1800s, the locality became a prime area for trappers and mountain men to travel through, one example being John Colter.
TodayOther mountain men who visited the valley include Jim Bridger, Jedediah Smith, and William Sublette, who are responsible for many of the names in the area.
