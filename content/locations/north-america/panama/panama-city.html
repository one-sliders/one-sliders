City history

1519The city was founded on 15 August 1519, by Pedro Arias de Ávila, also known as Pedrarias Dávila.
1520In 1520, Genoese merchants controlled the port and the commerce of the region, mainly for the slave trade, thanks to a concession given by the Spaniards, who had the Republic of Genoa as its.
1671In 1671 Henry Morgan with a band of 1,400 men attacked and looted the city, which was subsequently destroyed by fire.
1673The city was rebuilt in 1673 in a new location approximately 5 miles (8 km) southwest of the original city.
1848Between 1848 and 1869, the year the first transcontinental railroad was completed in the United States, about 375,000 people crossed the isthmus from the Atlantic to the Pacific, and 225,000 in the opposite direction.
1855One year before the start of the California Gold Rush, the Panama Railroad Company was formed, but the railroad did not begin full operation until 1855.
1903After declaring independence from Colombia on 3 November 1903, Philippe Bunau-Varilla (a French engineer) was named Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary.
1914Completed in 1914, the Panama Canal symbolized U.S. technological prowess and economic power.
1900sAs a result, a portion of the El Chorrillo neighborhood, which consisted mostly of old wood-framed buildings dating back to the 1900s (though still a large slum area), was destroyed by fire.
1970sIn the late 1970s through the 1980s the city of Panama became an international banking center, bringing a lot of undesirable attention as an international money-laundering locale.
Regional rolePanama City is the fourth earliest-founded of the modern-day capital cities in the Americas.
Regional roleHowever, most of the laborers for the construction of the canal were brought in from the Caribbean, which created unprecedented racial and social tensions in the city.
Regional roleDuring World War II, construction of military bases and the presence of larger numbers of U.S. military and civilian personnel brought about unprecedented levels of prosperity to the city.
Regional roleShipping is handled through port facilities in the area of Balboa operated by the Hutchison Whampoa Company of Hong Kong and through several ports on the Caribbean side of the isthmus.
