Barbados

History

1958The territory became part of the West Indies Federation, shaping its later political and regional identity.
1966Barbados became independent as a Commonwealth realm, keeping the monarch as head of state at first.
1966Barbados was admitted to the United Nations, confirming broad international recognition.
1973Barbados joined CARICOM at the Treaty of Chaguaramas, tying the country more closely to that regional or international organisation.
2021A republic was declared, changing Barbados' political system and state identity.
