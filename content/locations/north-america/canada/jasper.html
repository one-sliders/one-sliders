City history

170The Alberta and federal governments supplied $170 million in funding to the town; $30 million went towards construction of temporary and permanent housing, and the rest went to Parks Canada for recovery work in.
1912The Canadian Northern Railway began service to its Jasper Park station in 1912, about 700 m from GTP's Fitzhugh station.
1916An internment camp of Ukrainian Canadians, suspected by virtue of their place of birth of having enemy loyalties, was in operation at Dominion Park in Jasper from February 1916 to August 1916.
1930Jasper Forest Park was renamed Jasper National Park in 1930.
1940In 1940, the scenic Icefields Parkway opened, connecting Jasper to Lake Louise and Banff in Banff National Park.
1995The first step towards incorporation of Jasper occurred on August 31, 1995, when the Jasper Improvement District was formed from a portion of Improvement District No.
2001The improvement district was subsequently incorporated as a specialized municipality under the name of the Municipality of Jasper on July 20, 2001.
2024The Jasper wildfire began on July 22, 2024 and destroyed 358 buildings, one-third of the town's structures, causing an estimated $1.23 billion in damages.
2025By May 2025, insurance companies had finished clearing debris, and Parks Canada had issued 100 permits to start rebuilding houses destroyed in the fire.
Civic roleIt was renamed Jasper after the former fur trade post.
Regional roleThe incorporation order established the Jasper townsite as the Town of Jasper and the surrounding balance of the specialized municipality as a rural service area that was deemed equivalent to a municipal district.
Regional roleThe high cost was partly due to Jasper's geographical isolation, which made transporting materials into the town and moving debris out more difficult.
