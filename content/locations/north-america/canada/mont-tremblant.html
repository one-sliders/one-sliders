City history

1872It was settled in 1872 by parish priest Antoine Labelle, leading to formal establishment of the parish in 1879.
1892A railway line from Montreal was completed to the village of Saint-Jovite in 1892, and extended to Lac Mercier in 1904.
1905In 1905 a hydroelectric dam was erected on the banks of the Ruisseau Clair (Clear River) and the Rivière-du-Diable (Devil's River) providing electricity initially for Saint-Jovite.
1939Constructed by Joseph Bondurant Ryan, the ski resort Mont-Tremblant Lodge began operation of their first chair lift in 1939.
1965His family sold the resort in 1965 to a consortium of investors.
2002In 2002 the four municipalities in the area merged, Ville Saint-Jovite, Paroisse de Saint-Jovite, Mont-Tremblant, and Lac-Tremblant-Nord, becoming the amalgamated Ville de Mont-Tremblant.
