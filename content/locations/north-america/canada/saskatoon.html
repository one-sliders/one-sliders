City history

1882In 1882, the Toronto-based Temperance Colonization Society was granted 21 sections of land straddling the South Saskatchewan River, between what is now Warman and Dundurn.
1883Founder John Neilson Lake apparently told multiple stories, including the most famous, where in August 1883 he was handed a handful of berries, told their name, and exclaimed, "Arise Saskatoon, Queen of the North!".
1885In 1885 the North-West Rebellion affected the tiny community in a variety of ways.
1903A town charter for the west side of the river was obtained in 1903, and this settlement adopted the name Saskatoon; the original townsite, which became a village that year, was renamed Nutana.
1906In 1906 Saskatoon became a city with a population of 4,500, which included the communities of Saskatoon, Riversdale and Nutana.
1955In 1955, a newly established community west of the city, Montgomery Place, was annexed, followed by the neighbouring town of Sutherland in 1956.
