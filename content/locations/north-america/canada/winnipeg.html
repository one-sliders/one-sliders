City history

Early settlementEstimates of the date of first settlement in the area range from 11,500 years ago for a site southwest of the present city to 6,000 years ago at the Forks.
1738Sieur de La Vérendrye built the first fur-trading post on the site in 1738, called Fort Rouge.
1809The North West Company built Fort Gibraltar in 1809, and the Hudson's Bay Company built Fort Douglas in 1812, both in the area of present-day Winnipeg.
1822Fort Gibraltar was renamed Fort Garry in 1822 and became the leading post in the region for the Hudson's Bay Company.
1826A flood destroyed the fort in 1826 and it was not rebuilt until 1835.
1873On 8 November 1873, Winnipeg was incorporated as a city, with the Selkirk settlement as its nucleus.
1881Winnipeg developed rapidly after the coming of the Canadian Pacific Railway in 1881.
1914However, the city faced financial difficulty when the Panama Canal opened in 1914.
1919After many arrests, deportations, and incidents of violence, the strike ended on 21 June 1919 when the Riot Act was read.
1920Proportional voting was also introduced in 1920, and used provincially until 1957.
1939The Depression ended after the start of World War II in 1939.
1950When the war ended, pent-up demand generated a boom in housing development, although building activity was checked by the 1950 Red River flood.
