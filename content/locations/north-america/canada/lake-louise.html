City history

1882A Nakoda guide took CPR workman Tom Wilson to the site in 1882, and Wilson named it 'Emerald Lake'.
1892Lake Louise was added to Rocky Mountains Park in 1892, and has subsequently become an international tourist destination, made more accessible by its location near the Trans-Canada Highway.
19101890 CPR station was replaced in 1910, but continued to be used by the railway company for other purposes until it was donated to Heritage Park Historical Village, Calgary, in 1976.
1991The 1910 station was declared a heritage railway station by the federal government in 1991, having been well-preserved and operating today as a restaurant.
1870sDuring the 1870s, construction of the Canadian Pacific Railway (CPR) began, with the route going through Bow Valley.
GrowthIn the language of the Nakoda people, Lake Louise is called Ho-run-num-nay, meaning "lake of the little fishes".
Civic roleThe community was later called 'Holt City', and then 'Laggan', before being renamed Lake Louise.
Regional roleThe depot was moved to the park, where it was restored in the style of the era when the village was called Laggan.
