History

1667Dutch control changed control of the territory.
1831British Guiana appears in Guyana's historical record.
1966Guyana became independent from the United Kingdom as Guyana, marking a key step in modern statehood.
1970Guyana became a republic, replacing the previous constitutional arrangement.
1973Guyana joined CARICOM at the Treaty of Chaguaramas, tying the country more closely to that regional or international organisation.
1980The current constitution came into force, setting the basic framework for today's Guyana.

Worth seeing

  • Kaieteur Falls: One of the world's most powerful waterfalls, hidden in untouched Amazonian jungle.
  • Rainforest Wildlife: 90% forested, home to jaguars, tapirs, harpy eagles, and giant river otters.
  • Mount Roraima: Tabletop mountain (tepui) inspiring Arthur Conan Doyle's The Lost World.
