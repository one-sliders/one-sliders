City history

400Stating that "The Union shall own, in the central plateau of the Republic, an area of 14,400 square kilometers, which will be demarcated in due course to establish the future federal capital." The government.
1827The idea of relocating Brazil's capital city was conceived in 1827 by José Bonifácio, an advisor to Emperor Pedro I.
1883According to a legend, Italian saint Don Bosco in 1883 had a dream in which he described a futuristic city that roughly fitted Brasília's location.
1891After the Proclamation of the Republic, the Third Article of the new Constitution from 1891 stated that the capital should be moved from Rio de Janeiro to a place close to the country's center.
1892Already in 1892, the astronomer Louis Cruls, in the service of the Brazilian government, had investigated the site for the future capital.
1922In 1922 to celebrate the 100th anniversary of Brazil's independence, President Epitácio Pessoa ordered the construction of an obelisk in the Cruls Quadrilateral, known as the Foundation Stone of Brasília, to mark the location.
1956Upon taking office in January 1956, in fulfilment of his campaign pledge, he initiated the planning and construction of the new capital.
20th centuryThough automobiles were invented prior to the 20th century, mass production of vehicles in the early 20th made them widely available; thus, they became a symbol of modernity.
Modern cityBrasilia's geographically central location fostered a more regionally neutral federal capital.
Civic roleHe presented a plan to the General Assembly of Brazil for a new city called Brasília, with the idea of moving the capital westward from the heavily populated southeastern corridor.
