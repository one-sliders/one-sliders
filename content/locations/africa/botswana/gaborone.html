City history

1880In more recent history, the Tlokwa left the Magaliesberg ranges to settle in the area around 1880, and called the settlement Moshaweng.
1890In 1890, Cecil John Rhodes picked Gaberones to house a colonial fort.
1964The modern town was only founded in 1964, after a decision was taken to establish a capital for Botswana, which became a self governing territory in 1965, before becoming a fully independent republic on.
1965In 1965, the capital of the Bechuanaland Protectorate moved from Mafeking to Gaberones.
1966On 30 September 1966, Bechuanaland became the eleventh British dependency in Africa to become independent.
1969The city changed its name from Gaberones to Gaborone in 1969.
1971In 1971, because of the growth of illegal settlements, the Gaborone Town Council and the Ministry of Local Government and Lands surveyed an area called Bontleng, which would contain low-income housing.
1973In 1973, the Botswana Housing Corporation (BHC) built a "New Naledi" across the road from the "Old Naledi".
Civic roleGaberones, a shortening of "Gaborone's Village", was named after Chief Gaborone of the Tlokwa, whose home village (now called Tlokweng) was across the river from the Government Camp, the name of the colonial government.
Regional roleWhen Botswana gained its independence, Lobatse was the first choice as the nation's capital.
Regional roleHowever, Lobatse was deemed too limited, and instead, a new capital city would be created next to Gaberones.
Regional roleThe city was chosen because of its proximity to a fresh water source, its proximity to the railway to Pretoria, its central location among the central tribes, and its lack of association with those.
Regional roleBecause the town was built so quickly, there was a massive influx of labourers who had built illegal settlements on the new city's southern industrial development zone.
Regional roleThis has led to many squatter settlements on undeveloped land.
