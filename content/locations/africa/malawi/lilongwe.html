City history

1902Lilongwe was first set up as a boma by the local leader Njewa around 1902, and later became an administrative centre in 1904.
1947As a trading post, Lilongwe was officially recognized as a town in 1947.
1955The first plan for Lilongwe was published in 1955, before the decision was made in 1965 to move the capital from Zomba to Lilongwe.
1968Consultants were appointed to prepare the Lilongwe Master Plan, which was completed in 1968.
1975Lilongwe became the capital of Malawi in 1975, replacing the previous capital, Zomba.
1986The 1986 Outline Zoning Scheme was intended to promote regulated urban development and appropriate land use for transport and other purposes.
2011On 20 July 2011, the report on the Study of Urban Development Master Plan for Lilongwe City was approved by the Minister of Lands, Housing and Urban Development.
2012The Project for Urban Plan and Development Management of Lilongwe City has been in the process of implementation since November 2012, with support from JICA.
1920sIn the 1920s, its location at the junction of several major roadways increased its importance as an agricultural market centre for the fertile Central Region Plateau.
1970sDevelopment projects of the 1970s and 1980s included the construction of Lilongwe International Airport, which serves the city; rail connections to Salima to the east and the Zambian border to the west; industrial areas.
TodayAfter gaining independence, it increasingly developed into an important trading centre in Malawi's central region.
Regional roleThis incorporated boundary changes and new planning work on Lumbadzi and the Kamuzu International Airport.
Regional roleThe Lilongwe Outline Zoning Scheme was established and indicated the various land uses of the new capital city.
Regional roleUnplanned settlements occupied by illegal settlers have expanded in almost all areas.
