City history

1898In 1898, Arthur Church was commissioned to design the first town layout for the railway depot.
1899The railway arrived at Nairobi on 30 May 1899, and soon Nairobi replaced Machakos as the headquarters of the provincial administration for the Ukamba province.
1907In 1907, Nairobi replaced Mombasa as the capital of the East Africa Protectorate.
1926In February 1926, colonial officer Eric Dutton passed through Nairobi on his way to Mount Kenya, and said of the city: Maybe one day Nairobi will be laid out with tarred roads, with avenues.
1950In the spring of 1950, the East African Trades Union Congress (EAUTC) led a nine-day general strike in the city. Post independence Nairobi remained Kenya's capital after independence, and its continued rapid growth put.
1950sThis led to the Mau Mau Uprising in the 1950s, and the Lancaster House Conferences, which initiated a transition to Kenyan independence in 1963.
1972In 1972, the World Bank approved funds for further expansion of the then Nairobi Airport (now Jomo Kenyatta International Airport), including a new international and domestic passenger terminal building, the airport's first dedicated cargo.
1973In September 1973, the Kenyatta International Conference Centre (KICC) was opened to the public.
1978On 14 March 1978, construction of the terminal building was completed on the other side of the airport's single runway and opened by President Jomo Kenyatta less than five months before his death.
1983The Giraffe Centre, an animal sanctuary on the southwestern outskirts of Nairobi, was opened in 1983.
21st centuryIt is now the site of a memorial park. 21st century In October 2011, a memorial statue was unveiled in Nairobi in memory of Tom Mboya, a former Kenyan Independence politician and assassination victim.
2012In November 2012, President Mwai Kibaki opened the KES 31 billion Thika Superhighway.
1900sIn the early 1900s, Bazaar Street, now Biashara Street, was completely rebuilt after an outbreak of plague and the burning of the original town.
Regional roleThe airport was renamed Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in memory of its first president.
