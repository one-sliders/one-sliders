1815In 1815 HMS Comus, as part of the British blockade of Africa, sailed into Duke Town, where she captured seven Spanish and Portuguese slave ships.
1845Among the missionaries, Hope Waddell, who worked in Calabar from 1845 to 1858, and Mary Slessor, who evangelized Christianity in Calabar from 1876 to 1915, worked to improve treatment by and among the native.
1846In 1846, Scottish Presbyterians established a mission station in Calabar.
1884On 10 September 1884, Queen Victoria signed a treaty of protection with the king and chiefs of Akwa Akpa, known to Europeans as Old Calabar—then the official title to distinguish it from New Calabar.
1906Calabar was the headquarter of the European administration in the Niger Delta until 1906 when the seat of government was moved to Lagos.
1923Herbert Macaulay, a newspaper owner and grandson of Samuel Ajayi Crowther, in 1923 founded the first Nigerian political party, the Nigeria National Democratic Party.
1926In 1926, Governor Graeme Thomson attempted to introduce a poll tax in southeast Nigeria, including Calabar.

1967The Southeastern area decided to become independent and declared itself as the Republic of Biafra in 1967.
