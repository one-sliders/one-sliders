City history

1863Explorers and campaigners Samuel Baker and Florence Baker used the nearby island of Gondokoro as a base during their expeditions to what is now South Sudan and northern Uganda from 1863 to 1865 and.
1899From the establishment of Anglo-Egyptian Sudan in 1899, the British administered southern Sudan separately from the north.
1927Major construction of the new city of Juba was underway by 1927.
1929Traders from Rejaf relocated to the new city in 1929, and the Governor's office of Mongalla was moved there in 1930.
1946In 1946, without consulting Southern opinion, the British administration began instead to implement a policy of uniting the north and the south.
1955In 1955, a mutiny of southern soldiers in Torit sparked the First Sudanese Civil War, which did not end until 1972.
1956Until 1956, Juba was in Anglo-Egyptian Sudan, which was jointly administered by the United Kingdom and the Kingdom of Egypt.
2011Juba became South Sudan's national capital on 9 July 2011, when South Sudan formally declared its independence from the Republic of the Sudan.
2017In November 2017, the former chief of general staff Paul Malong Awan was removed from the city, and since then has become a fortified stronghold for President Kiir.
1920sIn the late 1920s, Anglo-Egyptian officials ordered Bari residents to relocate so that a new town could be constructed to serve as the capital of Mongalla Province.
Regional rolePublic buildings, such as the Ivory Bank, Notos Lounge, the old Sudan Airways Building, Paradise Hotel, and the Nile Commercial Bank and Buffalo Commercial Bank, were all built by Greeks.
Regional roleUnder the leadership of the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs, the United Nations established a camp known as "OCHA Camp", which served as a base for many United Nations agencies.
Regional roleHowever, influential parties including the South Sudanese government expressed dissatisfaction with the city's suitability as a national capital, and the government proposed that a new planned city be built as a replacement capital elsewhere.
