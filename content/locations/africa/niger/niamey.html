City history

1897The first European mentions of Niamey were by the 1897 mission of Émile Auguste Léon Hourst, then the 1899 Voulet–Chanoine Mission, which set fire to Niamey.
1904Niamey had about 1,800 inhabitants in 1904, when it became the capital of the Third Military Territory of Niger.
1911The capital moved back to Zinder in 1911, after violence in the eastern region had subsided and reorganisation had drawn Niamey further away from the center of the territory.
1930As capital, Niamey also became the center for the educated class of mostly Songhai and Zarma people, with the first regional school opening in 1930.
1935Most residents wished to stay on the river bank, but, after a 1935 fire destroyed many straw buildings, they were forcibly moved to mudbrick houses, resulting in multiethnic neighbourhoods.
1966Despite this, housing insecurity increased, and the informal settlement of Talladjé was created in 1966.
1999On 9 April 1999, Baré was ambushed, shot, and killed at Niamey's airport during a coup d'état.
2013In 2013, American and French military bases were established at adjacent parts of Diori Hamani International Airport.
OriginsBackground and foundation The area of modern Niamey was of little importance before Niger's colonial era.
Civic roleThe villages of Goudel and Gamkalé were founded on the left bank (east side) of the Niger River in the sixteenth century.
Regional roleThose who remained established informal settlements with mostly thatched mud buildings on the edge of the indigenous quarter.
Regional roleMost of the urban plan was cancelled during the Second World War, when colonial officials left for the war and the administration prioritised commercial farming over infrastructure.
Regional roleNiamey received the country's only asphalt highway, spanning a few kilometres between Diori Hamani International Airport and the Presidential Palace—the former governor's palace.
Regional roleThis plan redrew municipalities' boundaries based on pre-colonial settlements, with consulting from traditional chiefs.
