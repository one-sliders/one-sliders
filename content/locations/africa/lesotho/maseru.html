City history

1869Maseru was founded by the British as a small police camp in 1869, following the conclusion of the Free State–Basotho Wars when Basutoland became a British protectorate.
1871During their rule between 1871 and 1884, Basutoland was treated similarly to territories that had been forcefully annexed, much to the chagrin of the Basotho.
1881This led to the Gun War in 1881 and the burning of many buildings in Maseru.
1884In 1884, Basutoland was restored to its status as a Crown colony, and Maseru was again made capital.
1966When Basutoland gained its independence and became the Kingdom of Lesotho in 1966, Maseru remained the country's capital.
1998After the 1998 parliamentary elections in Lesotho led to suspicions of vote fraud and a military intervention by South Africa, much of the city was damaged by riots and pillaging.
2008The cost of repairing the damage done to the city was estimated at two billion rand (US$350 million), and until almost 2008, the effects of the riots could still be seen within the city.
Regional rolePrior to Lesotho's independence, Maseru had remained relatively small; it was contained within well-defined colonial boundaries and had little room for growth, while the British had little interest in developing the city.
