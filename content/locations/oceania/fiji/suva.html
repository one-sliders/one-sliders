City history

575More than a tenth of this land area, 575 km2 (222 sq mi), was near Suva.
1868In 1868, when Suva was still a small village, the Bauan chieftain, Seru Epenisa Cakobau, granted 5,000 km2 (1,900 sq mi) of land to the Australian-based Polynesia Company, in exchange for the company's promise.
1874In 1874, control of the Fiji Islands was ceded to the United Kingdom.
1875Pratt of the Royal Engineers was appointed Surveyor-General in 1875 and designed the new capital in Suva, assisted by W.
1877In 1877, the colonial authorities moved the capital to Suva from Levuka, Ovalau, Lomaiviti, because Levuka's location between a steep mountain and the sea made any expansion of the town impractical.
1882The transfer of the administration to Suva was made official in 1882.
1910In 1910, Suva acquired the status of a municipality, under the Municipal Constitution Ordinance of 1909.
1952Its area remained one square mile until 1952, when Suva annexed the Muanikau and Samabula wards, expanding its territory to 13 km2 (5.0 sq mi).
2003Suva hosted the South Pacific Games in 2003 for the third time in the event's 40-year history.
Regional roleIn October of that year, Suva was officially designated a city – Fiji's first.
Regional roleMost recently, Suva further extended its boundaries by incorporating the Cunningham area at its northern edge.
Regional roleIt has a mix of modern buildings and colonial-period architecture.
