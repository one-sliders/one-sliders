City history

Early settlementThe Brisbane region has been inhabited for more than 22,000 years by the Yagara (Yuggera), Turrbal and Quandamooka peoples.
1824The first outpost was founded at Redcliffe in 1824 before relocating to North Quay in 1825.
1838Brisbane grew as a river port serving pastoral districts and the Moreton Bay islands, while German and Scottish migrants established early agricultural settlements, notably the Zion Hill Mission at Nundah in 1838 and Fortitude.
1842The settlement closed in 1842, opening the district to free colonisation.
1843The Brisbane district became a major front of the War of Southern Queensland (1843–1855), involving coordinated resistance from Yuggera and Turrbal groups, with support from neighbouring Ningy Ningy people, under leaders such as Dundalli.
1859Brisbane became the capital of the newly separated Colony of Queensland in 1859.
1864The city suffered two destructive fires in 1864, which destroyed much of the early commercial centre and led to new building regulations favouring brick over timber construction.
