City history

15th centuryThe island of Pohnpei was encountered by the Portuguese and Spanish in the 15th century, but colonial settlement did not occur until 1886 by the Spanish.
1898Later Germany attained the Caroline Islands via purchase from Spain at the end of the Spanish–American War in 1898.
1979Subsequent to World War II, the island was administered by the United States Government until FSM attained independence in 1979.
1989It was declared the capital of Micronesia in 1989.
Regional roleDuring World War II, the Japanese built an airport near Kolonia.
Regional roleThe government of the Federated States of Micronesia decided to convert the Palikir area into a town to house the central administration of the islands as a purpose built capital city.
Regional roleAll government offices, some residential houses, and the residence of the President of the Federated States of Micronesia were built.
