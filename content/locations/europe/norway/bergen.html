City history

1070The city of Bergen was traditionally thought to have been founded by king Olav Kyrre, son of Harald Hardråde in 1070 AD, four years after the Viking Age in England ended with the Battle.
1198In 1198, the Bagler faction set fire to the city in connection with a battle against the Birkebeiner faction during the civil war.
1299The functions of the capital city were lost to Oslo during the reign of King Haakon V (1299–1319).
1413In 1413 another fire struck the city, and 14 churches were destroyed.
1665In 1665, the city's harbour was the site of the Battle of Vågen, when an English naval flotilla attacked a Dutch merchant and treasure fleet supported by the city's garrison.
1702Accidental fires sometimes got out of control, and one in 1702 reduced most of the town to ashes.
1813The Bergen stock exchange, the Bergen børs, was established in 1813.
1838It was established as a municipality on 1 January 1838 (see formannskapsdistrikt).
1940During World War II, Bergen was occupied on the first day of the German invasion on 9 April 1940, after a brief fight between German ships and the Norwegian coastal artillery.
1944On 20 April 1944, during the German occupation, the Dutch cargo ship Voorbode anchored off the Bergenhus Fortress, loaded with over 120 tons of explosives, and blew up, killing at least 150 people and.
2020Today, Bergen is part of Vestland county, which was created in 2020 after the merger of Hordaland and Sogn og Fjordane. Fires The city's history is marked by numerous great fires.
1020sModern research has, however, discovered that a trading settlement had already been established in the 1020s or 1030s.
13th centuryBergen gradually assumed the function of capital of Norway in the early 13th century, as the first city where a rudimentary central administration was established.
14th centuryDuring the 14th century, North German merchants, who had already been present in substantial numbers since the 13th century, founded one of the four Kontore of the Hanseatic League at Bryggen in Bergen.
