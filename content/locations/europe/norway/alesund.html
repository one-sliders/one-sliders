City history

1793In 1793, the port of Aalesund was granted limited ladested rights.
1838On 1 January 1838, the new formannskapsdistrikt law went into effect, granting limited local self-government to all parishes in Norway.
1848In 1848, it was upgraded to the status of a kjøpstad, a more important market town.
1904In the night of 23 January 1904, the town was the scene of the Ålesund Fire, one of the most terrible of the many conflagrations to which Norwegian towns, once built largely of wood.
OriginsLegend has it that Gangerolf (outside of Norway better known as Rollo), the 10th-century founder of the dynasty of the dukes of Normandy, hailed from the island of Giske, north-west of the present-day town.
1960sDuring the 1960s, there were many municipal mergers across Norway due to the work of the Schei Committee.
GrowthAt least three statues of Rolle exist: in the town park in Ålesund, in the city of Rouen, France, and in Fargo, North Dakota, United States.
