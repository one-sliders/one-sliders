City history

1973The first rock carvings at Jiepmaluokta near Alta were discovered, leading to wider documentation of the site.
1985The rock art of Alta was placed on the UNESCO World Heritage List as Norway's only prehistoric World Heritage Site.
1991Alta Museum moved to the rock carving area and became the main visitor centre for the World Heritage site.
1993Alta Museum received the European Museum of the Year Award after developing the rock art visitor centre.
c. 4200 BCThe oldest phases of Alta's rock carvings date to the Stone Age, showing deep human activity around the Altafjord.
TodayModern Alta is a Finnmark hub for rock art, Sami culture, northern lights travel, airport access and Arctic routes.
