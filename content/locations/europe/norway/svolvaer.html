City history

429Initially, the new municipality had 2,429 residents and it included 7.3 square kilometres (2.8 sq mi) of land.
GrowthVågar is mentioned in the book Heimskringla, and might have been established as early as the year 800 AD.
1918The arms were designed by John Johnsen. Municipal self-government 1918-1963 Svolvær's historical significance as an important fishing village allowed the town to be granted town status (ladested) on 1 July 1918.
1964Due to this merger in 1964, Svolvær lost its status as a "town" (ladested).
1996Following new legislation in Norway in 1996, the municipal council of Vågan Municipality voted to make Svolvær a "town" once again.
OriginsThe first town formation known in North Norway, Vågar, was situated around the narrow, natural harbor near Kabelvåg, just west of Svolvær.
1960sDuring the 1960s, there were many municipal mergers across Norway due to the work of the Schei Committee.
Modern cityAtlantic Cod fisheries, particularly during winter months, have remained one of the most important economical foundations for the town.
TodayOther industries which have proved to be valuable resources for Vågan are fish farming (salmon), Secora and Lofotkraft. Name The town (and former municipality) is named after the old Svolvær farm (Old Norse: Svǫlver).
Regional roleThe official blazon is "Azure, a cod naiant argent in between a bar gemel wavy" (Norwegian: På blå bunn en vannrett sølv torsk mellom to bølgede strenger av sølv).
Regional roleThe blue color and the design of the arms symbolize the importance of the sea and fishing for the municipality.
Regional roleThe municipality is governed by a municipal council of directly elected representatives.
