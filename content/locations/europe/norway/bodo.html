City history

1959The current version which is a simplified, modern version of the old arms was granted on 24 July 1959.
OriginsThe municipality is named after the old Bodøgård farm (Old Norse: Boðvin), since the town was built on its ground.
GrowthThe first element might be boði which means "sunken rock" or "skerry" and the last element is vin which means "meadow" or "pasture".
Modern cityIt showed a midnight sun above a boat on the sea in front of a mountain range.
Civic roleThe official blazon is "Gules, a sun Or" (Norwegian: På rød bunn en gull sol).
Regional roleThe sun was chosen since Bodø was the first town in Northern Norway, and it experiences the midnight sun every year.
