City history

800A royal centre is thought to have existed at Oddernes before 800, and the church was built around 1040.
1635In 1635, King Christian IV ordered his feudal seigneur, Palle Rosenkrantz, to move from Nedenes and build a royal palace on the island. Foundation to 1900 Christian IV (renowned for having founded many towns).
1666In 1666, Christianssand became a garrison town and was heavily fortified.
1734Christianssand experienced its first fire in 1734, which was devastating to the city.
1804The City of Kristiansand had a quarantine station for maritime traffic and hospital at Odderøy Island for cholera patients that opened in 1804.
1880It burned buildings as far as the cathedral, which had been rebuilt in brick after a previous fire in 1880. 1900 onward With the development of hydropower in southern Norway, the city gradually developed.
19th centuryAnother important development during the 19th century was the foundation in 1881 of Eg Sindssygeasyl, the second central psychiatric institution in Norway (after Gaustad).
1892The most recent major fire, in 1892, left half the original section of the city in ashes.
1980sIn the 1980s, industry and business in the city declined, in part because of the 1986 fire at the Hotel Caledonien.
16th centuryAnother important element in the development of Kristiansand was the harbor on the island of Flekkerøy, which was the most important on the Skagerrak beginning in the 16th century and was first fortified under.
TodayGrauthelleren (Grathelleren), located on Fidjane, is believed to be a Stone Age settlement.
Regional roleArchaeological excavations to the east of Oddernes Church have uncovered rural settlements that existed during the centuries immediately before and after the start of the common era.
Regional roleTogether with a corresponding discovery in Rogaland, these settlements are unique in the Norwegian context; isolated farms, rather than villages, were the norm in ancient Norway.
Regional roleBefore the stone church was built, one or perhaps two wooden post churches are believed to have stood on the same spot.
