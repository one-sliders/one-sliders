1263The first prince of Moscow was Daniel, the youngest son of Alexander Nevsky, and in 1263, he was given Moscow as an otchina (hereditary land), where he established a local branch of Rurikid princes.
1326The foundation of Moscow's first stone church, the Dormition Cathedral, was laid in 1326, and the metropolitan chose to be buried there – an act that would cement Moscow's status as the spiritual center.
