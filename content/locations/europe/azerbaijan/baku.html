City history

GrowthIt was called Bagavan during the Sassanian rule (22-651 CE). and later became known by its Perso-Arabic name باکو (Bākū).
1170Akhsitan I built a navy in Baku and successfully repelled a Rus' assault in 1170.
1191After a devastating earthquake struck Shamakhi, the capital of Shirvan, Shirvanshah's court moved to Baku in 1191.
1797In March 1797 the tsarist troops left Baku, and the city became part of Qajar Iran again.
1812In 1812, a city court was established where local customs and Russian law were applied together.
1840With the transition to civilian administration in 1840, Baku became a district center attached to the Caspian Province.
1859Following the devastating earthquake in Shamakhi in 1859, the city gained the status of a provincial capital and emerged as one of the major administrative and political centers of the Caucasus.
1911Williams Jackson of Columbia University wrote in his work From Constantinople to the Home of Omar Khayyam (1911): Baku is a city founded upon oil, for to its inexhaustible founts of naphtha it owes.
1918Seeking to capitalize on the existing ethnic conflicts, by spring 1918, Bolsheviks inspired and condoned civil war in and around Baku.
