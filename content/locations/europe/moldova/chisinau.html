City history

795The epicenter of the quake was in the Vrancea Mountains, and it led to substantial destruction: 78 deaths and 2,795 damaged buildings (of which 172 were destroyed).
1840In 1840, the building of the Triumphal Arch, planned by the architect Luca Zaushkevich, was completed.
1875Chișinău-Ungheni-Iași railway was opened on 1 June 1875 in preparation for the Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878).
1905On 22 August 1905, another violent event occurred: the police opened fire on an estimated 3,000 demonstrating agricultural workers.
1933In 1933, the first higher education institution in Bessarabia was established by transferring the Agricultural Sciences Section of the University of Iași to Chișinău, as the Faculty of Agricultural Sciences. World War II On.
1940Following the Soviet occupation, mass deportations, linked with atrocities, were executed by the NKVD between June 1940 and June 1941.
1941In June 1941, in order to recover Bessarabia, Romania entered World War II under the command of the German Wehrmacht, declaring war on the Soviet Union.
1951The share of dwellings built during the Soviet period (1951–1990) represents 74.3 percent of total households.
1977On 4 March 1977, the city was again jolted by a devastating earthquake.
2022On 5 September 2022, the country's first Christian university, Universitatea Moldo-Americană, opened its doors, supported by the Scandinavian broadcaster Visjon Norge and several donors in Norway, and run in cooperation with the American Southeastern.
2023In April 2023, the Dutch government opened a new embassy in Chișinău.
Regional roleUnder the Russian government, Chișinău became the capital of the newly annexed oblast (later guberniya) of Bessarabia.
Regional roleRomania granted important subsidies to its provinces and initiated large-scale investment programs in the infrastructure of the main cities in Bessarabia, expanded the railroad infrastructure, and launched an extensive program to eradicate illiteracy.
Regional roleDuring this time, Chișinău, part of Lăpușna County, was the capital of the newly established Bessarabia Governorate of Romania.
