City history

10821082, second in Sweden (within its pre-1658 boundaries) only to Skara.
1164On several occasions attempts to achieve a separate Swedish archdiocese were based in Linköping, although, when they finally were successful in 1164, Uppsala was chosen instead.
1251The coronation of Valdemar Birgersson took place in the cathedral in 1251.
1266A cathedral school can be traced from 1266.
1600This ultimately led to the rise to the throne of Charles (de facto at the 1600 Riksdag of the Estates meeting in Linköping and formally four years later) and the end of the short-lived.
1627In 1627 the current Linköping cathedral school was established, making it the third oldest gymnasium in Sweden.
1937Linköping was a small town until 1937, when the Saab aircraft industry was formed, starting a period of rapid expansion.
2022In 2022, a rare Viking grave was found in Linköping that seemed to mark the Viking transition from paganism to Christianity.
OriginsThe city is possibly named after the Lionga ting assembly which according to Medieval Scandinavian laws was the most important thing in Östergötland.
Regional roleIn the reign of Gustavus Vasa several important diets were held in the town.
