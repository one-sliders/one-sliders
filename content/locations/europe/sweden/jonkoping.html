City history

1284On 18 May 1284 Jönköping became the first City in Sweden to be granted its rights by king Magnus Ladulås, who ruled mostly from Vättern's largest island Visingsö.
1831The phosphorus match was invented in 1831, and these matches became very popular because one could strike it against any surface to ignite it.
1844In 1844, Swedish professor Gustav Erik Pasch patented a new invention, "Safety matches – Strike against the box only".
1845Jönköping was known for its matchstick industry between 1845 and 1970.
2001Since 2001, Elmia has been the site of the world's largest LAN party, DreamHack, with two events every year, Dreamhack Summer and Dreamhack Winter.
Regional roleThe city was plundered and burned several times until it was fortified during the 16th and 17th centuries.
Regional roleTo prevent the matches from igniting so easily, Gustav Erik Pasch separated the chemicals in the match head and placed the phosphorus on a separate surface on the outside of the box for striking.
Regional roleLater, they manufactured their new Safety matches in their factory in Jönköping.
Regional roleToday, the Match Museum is located in Jönköping's first match factory. Present The urban area of Jönköping today includes the eastern industrial town of Huskvarna, with which it has grown together.
Regional roleElmia, a major trade fair and exhibition centre, is situated in Jönköping.
Regional roleElmia Wood is the world's largest forestry fair, and fairs for subcontractors, trucks, caravans and railways are the biggest of their kind in Europe.
Regional roleThe city is an important Nordic logistical center, with many companies' central warehouses (such as Elgiganten, IKEA, Electrolux and Husqvarna) situated there.
Regional roleJönköping has one of the highest hotel and restaurant densities in Sweden.
