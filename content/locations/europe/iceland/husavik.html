City history

c. 870According to Landnamabok, Swedish Viking Gardar Svavarsson wintered at Husavik around 870.
c. 870Nattfari and two enslaved people were left behind when Gardar departed, creating an early farm settlement.
1907Husavikurkirkja, the town's well-known wooden church, was built and became a key local landmark.
2004Until 2004, Husavik served as an export harbour for silica extracted from the Lake Myvatn area.
NameThe name Husavik means bay of houses, probably referring to Gardar's homestead at the site.
TodayModern Husavik is shaped by fishing, tourism, whale watching, GeoSea baths and its harbour on Skjalfandi bay.
