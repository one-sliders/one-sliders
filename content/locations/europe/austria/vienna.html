City history

100-200The Roman military settlement of Vindobona stood on the site of today's central Vienna.
1155The Babenberg rulers made Vienna their residence, helping the settlement grow into a regional political centre.
1278Habsburg rule began to shape Vienna's long role as an imperial residence and administrative capital.
1683The failed Ottoman siege of Vienna became a decisive moment in the city's early modern history.
1804Vienna became capital of the Austrian Empire, reinforcing its role as a centre of court culture, administration and music.
1867After the Austro-Hungarian Compromise, Vienna remained capital of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
1918After World War I, Vienna became capital of the new Austrian republic.
1920-1934The Red Vienna period expanded municipal housing and social infrastructure, leaving major housing estates across the city.
1938Nazi Germany annexed Austria; Vienna's Jewish community was persecuted and much of its cultural life was destroyed.
1945Vienna was heavily damaged near the end of World War II and then divided into Allied occupation sectors.
1955The Austrian State Treaty restored full sovereignty, and Vienna continued as Austria's federal capital.
1979The Vienna International Centre opened, strengthening the city's role as a base for international organizations.

Worth seeing

Schonbrunn Palace

Former Habsburg summer palace with grand state rooms, formal gardens and a long park axis west of the centre.

St. Stephen's Cathedral

Vienna's Gothic landmark in the Innere Stadt, useful as a central meeting point, skyline marker, tower climb and first-orientation stop for the old town.

Hofburg

The imperial palace complex anchors central Vienna with museums, courtyards, the Spanish Riding School, state buildings and easy walks toward the Ringstrasse.

Belvedere

Baroque palace and museum complex near the main station, known for Austrian art collections, formal gardens and a route that pairs well with Wieden.

Prater

Large public park with the Riesenrad ferris wheel, amusement rides and broad paths for an easier outdoor break.

MuseumsQuartier

Dense museum and culture district near the Ringstrasse, practical when weather, contemporary art, cafes or short transfer times matter during a city stay.
