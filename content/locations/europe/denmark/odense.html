City history

c. 2000 BCEArchaeological excavations in the vicinity show proof of settlement for over 4,000 years since at least the Stone Age.
988The territory, previously part of the vast Archbishopric of Hamburg, was created a Catholic diocese in 988.
1279Greyfriars Monastery (Gråbrødre Kloster) was founded by the Franciscans in 1279.
13th centuryIn 1720, Frederick IV ordered the rebuilding of Odense Palace, partly on the foundations of the 13th century St.
1796An 8 kilometre (5.0 mi) long, 7.5 metre (25 ft) deep canal from Odense Harbour to Odense Fjord was built between 1796 and 1806 to facilitate the growth of Odense as a port city.
1853In 1853 Denmark's first modern water and gas works were opened in Odense.
1865Commerce was further boosted by the building of a railway across Funen in 1865, and Odense became one of Denmark's largest rail junctions.
1924Odense Central Library was established in 1924 and removed to Odense station in 1995.
1944Odinstårnet was blown up by a Danish Nazi group in 1944 and has never been rebuilt.
1960sFrom the 1960s Odense increasingly became a cultural hub, with the establishment of a university in 1966 and the launching of trade shows, including the large Odense Congress Centre.
1988In 1988 a major national television network, TV2, was established in Odense.
12th centuryThe first church in Odense appears to have been St Mary's, probably built in the late 12th century.
18th centuryGlove production, which had begun in the 18th century, developed into one of the most important industries while the harbour facilities were further expanded.
Regional roleThe Vikings built numerous fortifications along the river banks to defend it against invaders coming in from the coast.
