City history

1931Though the San Marino Football Federation was formed in 1931, the Federation did not establish a national team until 1986, when a team representing the Federation played against the Canada U-23 team in an.
1988San Marino gained affiliation to FIFA and UEFA in 1988, allowing the team to participate in major championships.
1990San Marino's first match in a FIFA-sanctioned competition was against Switzerland on 14 November 1990 in a qualifier for the 1992 European Championships.
1998Even by Sammarinese standards, the qualification campaign for the 1998 FIFA World Cup was disappointing.
2000Qualification for Euro 2000 again resulted in defeats in every match.
2001In April 2001, San Marino gained their first-ever away point, drawing 1–1 with Latvia in Riga.
2002The team ended the 2002 FIFA World Cup qualifying group with a new best of three goals, though one of these came in a 10–1 defeat by Belgium.
2003A 2–2 draw against Liechtenstein in a friendly in August 2003 saw the team set a new national record for most goals scored in a match; only twice since, in a 2–3 loss to.
2004In the UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying, San Marino lost all eight matches, failing to score.
2006Results during qualification for the 2006 World Cup followed a similar vein to previous qualifying groups.
2007For example, in the fixture against the Republic of Ireland in February 2007, 2,500 of the 3,294 crowd were Irish supporters.
2008San Marino's opening UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying match resulted in a record 13–0 defeat at home by Germany on 6 September 2006.
2010In the qualification campaign for the 2010 FIFA World Cup, they lost all ten matches and failed to qualify.
2012The UEFA Euro 2012 qualifiers started in a similar way, the first nine matches all being defeats with an aggregate of 49 goals conceded and none scored, their best result being a one-goal loss.
