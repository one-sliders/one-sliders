City history

1278The settlement of Andorra la Vella has been the principal city of Andorra since 1278 when the French and the Episcopal co-princes agreed to joint suzerainty.
1702Its most notable building is the Casa de la Vall—constructed in the early sixteenth century—which has been the state's parliamentary house since 1702.
1991It also hosted both the 1991 and 2005 Games of the Small States of Europe.
1993In 1993, the country's first constitution formalised this parliamentary democracy with executive, legislative, and judicial branches located in Andorra la Vella.
2010The city also developed its skiing facilities, to the extent that Andorra la Vella was Andorra's applicant city for the XXI Olympic Winter Games, the 2010 Winter Olympics.
OriginsThe site of Andorra la Vella has been settled since prior to the Christian era—notably by the Andosin tribe from the late Neolithic.
1930sAfter political turmoil in the 1930s and an attempted coup by Boris Skossyreff, an informal democracy developed.
GrowthAndorra was part of the County of Urgell, one of the Catalan counties of the Marca Hispanica created by Charlemagne in the eighth century as a buffer from the Moorish settlers in the Iberian.
Regional roleAndorra la Vella was, during this period, the capital of a largely isolated and feudal lordship within the Principality of Catalonia, which retained its existence due to this principle of co-sovereignty.
Regional roleWell into the twentieth century, the area around Andorra la Vella remained largely forgotten.
Regional roleDuring this period, Andorra also developed as a tax haven, resulting in the construction of modern banking offices in Andorra La Vella.
Regional roleHowever, Andorra la Vella was not selected by the IOC as a candidate city, following the evaluation report of an IOC commission.
