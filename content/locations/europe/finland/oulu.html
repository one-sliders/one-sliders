City history

1323Following the Treaty of Nöteborg in 1323, the status of Oulu was disputed in its ownership, as both the Novgorod Republic and the Kingdom of Sweden held much influence.
1345In 1345, the Swedish annexed territory up to the Kemi River for the Diocese of Turku.
1375In 1375, the Novgorodians built a defensive castle which was later captured by the Swedish forces in 1377.
1531In 1531, the city was granted permission to act as a trading post.
1590In 1590, the Oulu Castle was built by the Swedes and in 1595, the Treaty of Teusina firmly established Sweden's control over Oulu.
1610The city was founded on 8 April 1605 by King Charles IX of Sweden, and granted city privileges in 1610.
1765In 1765, Oulu received township rights granting them to trade outside of Sweden.
1776Oulu was the capital of the Province of Oulu from 1776 to 2009.
1822In 1822, a major fire destroyed much of the city, especially the city centre as most of the city was made from wood.
1832The Oulu Cathedral was built in 1832 to his designs, with the spire being finished in 1844.
1918The city was the site of the Battle of Oulu on 3 February 1918 between the Whites and the Reds during the Finnish Civil War.
OriginsThe city is named after the river Oulujoki, which originates in the lake Oulujärvi.
Modern cityOne possible source is a word in the Sami language meaning 'flood water', but there are other suggestions.
Regional roleDuring the Åland War, part of the Crimean War, Oulu's harbour was raided by the British fleet, who destroyed ships and burned tar houses, leading to international criticism.
